Cipher encryption and decryption



ROT13 is a simple letter substitution cipher that replaces a letter with the 13th letter after it in the alphabet

The Atbash cipher is a very common and simple cipher that simply encodes a message with the reverse of the alphabet. Basically, when encoded, an "A" becomes a "Z", "B" turns into "Y", etc.

Vigenere cipher is a method of encrypting alphabetic text by using a series of interwoven Caesar ciphers, based on the letters of a keyword.
